Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 23, 2026

1. Overview & Commitment to Privacy

Airimg is designed around a zero-trust, client-side-first architecture. We do not operate central servers storing user images, personal credentials, or tracking telemetry. Your privacy and data ownership are our fundamental priorities.

2. Google OAuth & Drive Scope (`drive.file`)

Airimg connects to your Google Drive account using Google OAuth 2.0. We strictly request the narrow `https://www.googleapis.com/auth/drive.file` scope.

  • Airimg only has access to files created or uploaded by Airimg itself.
  • Airimg cannot read, list, modify, or delete any existing files or folders in your personal Google Drive.
  • Your OAuth access tokens are stored strictly in your browser's local storage and are never uploaded to any centralized database.

3. Client-Side Compression & EXIF Stripping

All image processing, WebP re-encoding, and EXIF metadata stripping occur directly inside your browser via HTML5 Canvas prior to upload.

  • Camera metadata, EXIF tags, timestamps, and GPS location coordinates are permanently removed client-side.
  • Uncompressed raw files never leave your local device. Only the processed WebP file is transmitted directly from your browser to Google Drive APIs.

4. Cloudflare Worker Edge Proxy

To serve public image hotlinks (`https://img.airimg.airlabs.eu.cc/i/`), Airimg utilizes a stateless Cloudflare Worker proxy layer.

  • The Worker acts solely as a stateless reverse proxy to stream public Google Drive image assets.
  • The Worker does not record user IP addresses, track request histories, or store persistent copies of your images.

5. Local Storage Vault

Airimg uses your browser's standard localStorage to store your upload history (Hotlink Vault) for convenience. This data remains on your local machine and can be cleared at any time using the "Clear All" button in the application interface.

6. Contact Information

If you have questions or feedback regarding this Privacy Policy, feel free to submit an issue on our official GitHub Repository.
